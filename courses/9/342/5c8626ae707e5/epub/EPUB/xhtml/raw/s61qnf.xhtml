Dental Caries (tooth decay):-

 

Any loss of tooth structure may be due to either:-

1-Bacterial causes: as dental caries.

2-Non bacterial causes: this is of three types:-

A-Mechanical cause

B-Chemical causes

C-Pathological cause

 

 

 

A-Mechanical cause:

includes

-abrasion: the wearing of teeth especially at the gingival third by wrong or over vigorous brushing.

-attrition: the wearing of tooth surface by the action of apposing teeth.

Attrition occurs with age or due to bruxism or with certain diets.

-abfraction: As teeth flex under pressure, the arrangement of teeth touching each other, known as occlusion, causes tension on one side of the tooth and compression on the other side of the tooth. This believed to cause V-shaped depressions on the side under tension and C-shaped depressions on the side under compression.
